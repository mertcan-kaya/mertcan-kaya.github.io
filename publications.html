  • Journal Article • 2026
    Motor interference of elbow configuration changes in human-robot interaction
    M. Kaya, et al.
    Interaction Studies, Vol. 27, No. 1, pp. 45–68.
    Publisher Link Preprint PDF
    View Abstract
    This work investigates the cross-modal behavioral adaptations executed by human participants when working in close proximity with collaborative redundant manipulators. Our empirical data shows that secondary joint re-orientations within the user's field of view induce involuntary path tracking errors and localized biomechanical compensation, pointing to the need for human-aware cost boundaries in inverse kinematics solvers.
  • Journal Article • 2024
    A novel recursive algorithm for the implementation of adaptive robot controllers
    M. Kaya, M. A. Akbulut, Z. Y. Bayraktaroglu, and K. Kühnlenz
    Journal of Intelligent & Robotic Systems, Vol. 110, Article No. 42.
    Publisher Link PDF Asset
    View Abstract
    Standard model-based adaptive control loops require calculation of high-dimensional regressor matrices, capping execution rates on industrial embedded processors. We present a novel recursive parameter tracking framework based on inverse Newton-Euler physics. By bypassing explicit regressor formations entirely, it cuts computation time, prevents lag, and allows microprocessors to run loop iterations at higher frequencies.

  • Zenodo Preprint • Under Active Review (2026)
    The Geometry of Interference: Quantifying Spatial Leakage and Biomechanical Adaptation During Human–Robot Collaboration
    M. Kaya and K. Kühnlenz
    Zenodo Repository Preprint Portal. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.placeholder.
    Access Preprint Page
    View Technical Contribution
    This paper formulates the Distractor-Aligned Variance (DAV) algorithm pipeline. Traditional metrics blend baseline task movements with accidental tracking jitters, failing to isolate pure behavioral disruption. By mapping errors orthogonal to the runtime task vector, DAV extracts human motor tracking noise from intentional movements. This serves as a clean input signal for real-time human-aware path planning loops.
