  • Journal Article • 2026
    Motor interference of elbow configuration changes in human-robot interaction
    M. Kaya, K. Becker, J. Greve, J. Keller, M. Meserle, C. Först, R. Siegel, J. Stelzer, and K. Kühnlenz
    Interaction Studies, 26(1), 130–149.
    DOI Publisher Link
    View Abstract
    This work explores the cross-modal behavioral adaptations executed by human participants when interacting in synchronous proximity with collaborative industrial redundant manipulators. Our statistical results prove that null-space orientation shifts within the user's field of view provoke subconscious joint trajectory corrections and localized biomechanical alignment actions across human tracking paths.

  • Journal Article • 2025
    Subjective task-load influences anthropomorphism during cooperative human and robot hand movements
    M. Kaya and K. Kühnlenz
    at - Automatisierungstechnik, 73(1), 22–28.
    DOI Publisher Link
    View Abstract
    This study isolates the psychological interactions between physical work boundaries and cognitive human attributions during synchronous hand tracking. The experimental results reveal that modulating structural task-loads significantly variations the baseline anthropomorphic scores assigned to standard industrial robotic configurations.

  • Journal Article • 2024
    A novel recursive algorithm for the implementation of adaptive robot controllers
    M. Kaya, M. A. Akbulut, Z. Y. Bayraktaroglu, and K. Kühnlenz
    Journal of Intelligent & Robotic Systems, 110(3), 115.
    DOI Publisher Link
    View Abstract
    We present an optimized, recursive formulation of model-based robot dynamics based on inverse Newton-Euler tracking strategies. By completely removing high-dimensional regressor matrix structural requirements, we eliminate computational matrix building latency and scale loop execution frequencies smoothly on production hardware microprocessors.

    M. Kaya and K. Kühnlenz
    Zenodo Repository Preprint Portal.
    DOI Preprint Link
    View Technical Summary
    Introduces the formal pipeline of Distractor-Aligned Variance (DAV) modeling. The algorithm isolates task-orthogonal trace leakage vectors from baseline planned target paths, extracting pure control noise to serve as a feedback primitive for reactive costmap generation.
