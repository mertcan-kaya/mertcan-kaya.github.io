Selected Publications & Core Formulations

🔬 View Mathematical Formulation: Distractor-Aligned Variance (DAV)

To mathematically isolate and track human motor interference profiles during physical or proximal collaboration, the framework filters out target task vectors to gauge trajectory variances orthogonal to the running task path trajectory \(\mathbf{x}(t)\):

$$\text{DAV} = \frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{T} \left\| \mathbf{e}(t) - \left( \mathbf{e}(t)^T \hat{\mathbf{v}}_d(t) \right) \hat{\mathbf{v}}_d(t) \right\|^2 dt$$
  • Journal Article • Interaction Studies • 2026
    Motor interference of elbow configuration changes in human-robot interaction
    Problem: Collaborative robots changing configuration orientations inside shared visual fields can trigger safety adjustments or cognitive load increases for human workers.
    Contribution: Mapped visual tracking data and joint trajectories to quantify human adjustments relative to secondary robot null-space positioning maneuvers.
    Outcome: Proved that null-space orientation shifts evoke subconscious joint adjustments, giving clear planning bounds for trajectory generator design.
  • Journal Article • Journal of Intelligent & Robotic Systems • 2024
    A novel recursive algorithm for the implementation of adaptive robot controllers
    Problem: Standard adaptive control models rely on calculating high-dimensional regressor structural matrices, creating computational limits for high-speed loops.
    Contribution: Formulated a recursive multi-body dynamic inversion mapping loop based on inverse Newton-Euler formulations.
    Outcome: Eliminated explicit regressor formulation bottlenecks entirely, cutting calculation overhead and scaling execution loop speeds on hardware microprocessors.
